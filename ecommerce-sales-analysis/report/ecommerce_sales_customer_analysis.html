Author: Raahul John Alex¶
Tools Used: Python, Pandas, Matplotlib, Jupyter Notebook¶

Project Overview¶

This project analyzes an e-commerce retail dataset to understand sales performance, customer behavior and product trends. The goal is to extract business insights that could help decision-makers improve revenue, optimize product strategy and better understand customer segments.

Business Problem¶

Retail companies generate large volumes of transactional data. However, without proper analysis, it becomes difficult to understand which products drive revenue, which regions perform best, and how customers contribute to sales.

This analysis aims to answer several key business questions:

  • What is the total revenue and profit generated?

  • Which regions generate the highest sales?

  • Which product categories perform best?

  • Who are the top customers?

  • How do sales trends evolve over time?

Understanding these patterns can help businesses improve sales strategy and customer targeting.

Profit vs Discount Analysis¶

Impact of Discounts on Profitability¶

Discounting is commonly used in retail to drive sales. However, excessive discounts can significantly reduce profit margins. This analysis examines how discounts affect profitability across orders.

Understanding this relationship can help businesses balance revenue growth and profitability.

In [18]:
plt.figure(figsize=(8,6))

plt.scatter(df["discount"], df["profit"], alpha=0.3)

plt.title("Profit vs Discount")
plt.xlabel("Discount")
plt.ylabel("Profit")

plt.show()

Insight¶

The scatter plot shows that higher discounts often lead to lower profits, and in many cases even negative profits. This suggests that aggressive discounting strategies may harm overall profitability and should be carefully managed.
